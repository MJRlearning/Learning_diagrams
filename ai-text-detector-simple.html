Can You Spot AI Generated Text?

Test your skills at identifying AI-generated news summaries

How to Play

Below are pairs of news summaries on various topics. For each pair, one summary was written by a human journalist, and one was generated by an AI. Read both carefully and try to identify which is which.

After making your decision, click the "Reveal Answer" button to see if you were correct. Use the clues below to help you spot AI-generated text.

Clues for Spotting AI-Generated Text
Formal language: AI tends to use more formal, academic language compared to human writers who often use more casual or conversational phrasing.
Generic descriptions: AI often uses vague, general descriptions while humans tend to include specific, vivid details.
Quote handling: AI often paraphrases or formalizes direct quotes rather than using the original colorful language.
Emotional distance: AI descriptions of emotional events often sound detached or clinical compared to human writing.
Unnecessary complexity: AI may use unnecessarily complex sentence structures and vocabulary when simpler ones would work better.
Clinical terminology: AI tends to use clinical or technical terms (e.g., "lacerations" instead of "cuts").
Wordiness: AI often uses more words to express what humans would say concisely.

How to tell: The AI-generated text (B) uses more formal language with phrases like "strategic pricing decision" and "significant price reduction" instead of the more direct "surprise price drop" in the human text. The AI text also avoids specific numbers (like the 2.3% stock drop) and uses more generic descriptive phrases like "slight decline" instead.
