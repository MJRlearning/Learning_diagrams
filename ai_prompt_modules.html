Module 1: Prompt Engineering Basics

What is Prompt Engineering?

Prompt engineering is the skill of crafting clear, specific instructions for AI tools to get the best possible responses. Think of it as learning a new language to communicate effectively with your AI teaching assistant.

Key Principle: The clearer and more specific your prompt, the better the AI's response will be.

Basic Prompt Structure

Every good prompt should include:

  • Role: Tell the AI what role to play
  • Task: Clearly state what you want
  • Context: Provide relevant background information
  • Format: Specify how you want the output

Example: Basic vs. Improved Prompt

Basic Prompt:
"Help me with a math lesson."
Improved Prompt:
"Act as a 5th-grade math teacher. Create a 20-minute lesson plan on fractions for students who are just learning the concept. Include 3 hands-on activities and provide a simple worksheet. Format this as a structured lesson plan with timing for each section."

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Avoid these common pitfalls:
  • Being too vague or general
  • Assuming the AI knows your context
  • Not specifying the desired format
  • Forgetting to mention your audience (age, level, etc.)

Module 2: Essential Techniques for Educators

The CLEAR Framework

Use this framework for education-focused prompts:

  • Context - What subject, grade level, student needs?
  • Learning Objective - What should students achieve?
  • Expectations - What format, style, or constraints?
  • Audience - Who are your students specifically?
  • Resources - What materials or time do you have?

Technique 1: Role Playing

Start your prompts by assigning the AI a specific educational role:

"Act as an experienced high school biology teacher..."
"You are a patient elementary school reading specialist..."
"Take on the role of a special education coordinator..."

Technique 2: Step-by-Step Instructions

Break complex requests into numbered steps:

"Create a science lesson following these steps:
1. Start with a hook question about weather
2. Explain the water cycle in simple terms
3. Include a hands-on experiment
4. End with assessment questions
5. Provide extension activities for advanced students"

Technique 3: Providing Examples

Show the AI what you want by providing examples:

"Create math word problems similar to this example:
'Sarah has 24 stickers. She gives 1/3 to her brother and 1/4 to her sister. How many stickers does Sarah have left?'

Make 5 more problems with the same structure but different scenarios and numbers."

Module 3: Classroom Applications

lesson Planning

Template Prompt for Lesson Plans:

"Act as a [GRADE LEVEL] [SUBJECT] teacher. Create a [DURATION] lesson plan on [TOPIC] for students who [PRIOR KNOWLEDGE LEVEL]. The lesson should include:
- Learning objectives
- Materials needed
- Step-by-step activities with timing
- Assessment strategies
- Differentiation for [SPECIFIC NEEDS]

Format as a structured lesson plan that I can follow easily."

Creating Assessments

Assessment Creation Template:

"Create a [TYPE OF ASSESSMENT] for [GRADE LEVEL] students on [TOPIC]. Include:
- [NUMBER] multiple choice questions with 4 options each
- [NUMBER] short answer questions
- 1 extended response question
- Answer key with explanations
- Rubric for the extended response

Align with [SPECIFIC STANDARDS] and ensure questions range from basic recall to higher-order thinking."

Differentiation Support

Differentiation Prompt:

"Take this lesson activity: [DESCRIBE ACTIVITY]

Create 3 versions:
1. Simplified version for struggling learners
2. Standard version for grade-level students
3. Extended version for advanced students

Maintain the same learning objective but adjust complexity, support, and expectations."

Parent Communication

Parent Email Template:

"Help me write a professional email to parents about [SITUATION/TOPIC]. The tone should be [TONE - supportive/informative/collaborative]. Include:
- Clear explanation of the situation
- Specific examples
- Action steps for parents
- Invitation for follow-up discussion

Keep it concise but thorough, and maintain a positive, solution-focused approach."

Module 4: Advanced Strategies

Chain of Thought Prompting

Ask the AI to show its thinking process, especially for complex educational problems:

"A student is struggling with reading comprehension. Walk me through your thinking process to identify possible causes and solutions. Start with 'Let me think through this step by step:' and show your reasoning at each stage."

Iterative Refinement

Use follow-up prompts to improve responses:

Initial prompt: "Create a science activity about plants."

Follow-up: "Make this more hands-on and include a data collection component."

Further refinement: "Adapt this for students with limited mobility."

Constraint-Based Prompting

Set specific limitations to get more targeted results:

"Create a vocabulary activity with these constraints:
- Must use only materials found in a typical classroom
- Should take exactly 15 minutes
- Must accommodate students with dyslexia
- Should incorporate movement
- Must assess understanding without written work"

Perspective Taking

Ask the AI to consider multiple viewpoints:

"Analyze this classroom management situation from three perspectives:
1. The teacher's perspective
2. The student's perspective
3. The parent's perspective

Then suggest a solution that addresses all three viewpoints."

Quick Reference Checklist

Before submitting any prompt, check:
  • Did I specify the AI's role?
  • Is my task clearly stated?
  • Did I provide enough context?
  • Did I specify the target audience?
  • Did I mention the desired format?
  • Are there any constraints or special requirements?
  • Would a colleague understand what I'm asking for?
