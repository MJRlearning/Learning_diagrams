Best Practices for Writing Effective Prompts

Based on research, here are some best practices for writing specific and effective prompts. Click on each practice to see an example.

1. Be clear and concise: Use straightforward language and avoid ambiguity. Clearly state what you want the AI to do or produce.
Example: "Write a 100-word summary of the plot of Romeo and Juliet."
2. Provide context: Give relevant background information, specify the target audience, and describe any important themes or considerations.
Example: "Explain the concept of blockchain to a group of high school students who have basic knowledge of computers but no experience with cryptocurrencies."
3. Use specific instructions: Instead of vague requests, give precise directions.
Example: "List five common symptoms of the flu, including their typical duration and severity."
4. Assign a role: When appropriate, give the AI a specific role to assume.
Example: "As a financial advisor, provide three tips for young adults looking to start investing in the stock market."
5. Break down complex tasks: If you have a multi-part request, separate it into clear steps or bullet points.
Example: "Please help me plan a vacation: 1. Suggest three budget-friendly European destinations. 2. For each destination, list two must-see attractions. 3. Provide an estimated daily budget for food and accommodation."
6. Include examples: Providing examples can help guide the AI towards the style or format you're looking for.
Example: "Write a haiku about spring. A haiku is a three-line poem with 5 syllables in the first line, 7 in the second, and 5 in the third. Here's an example: 'An old silent pond... A frog jumps into the pond, splash! Silence again.'"
7. Set constraints: Establish guidelines or limitations to refine the AI's output.
Example: "Write a 150-word product description for a new smartphone. Use a professional tone and highlight three key features. Do not mention the price."
8. Use positive language: Frame your prompts in terms of what you want, rather than what you don't want.
Example: Instead of "Don't use technical jargon," say "Use simple, everyday language."
9. Utilize punctuation: Use commas, quotation marks, and line breaks to help the AI parse complex prompts more effectively.
Example: "Write a short dialogue between two characters: - Character A: A busy executive - Character B: A laid-back artist Topic: The value of work-life balance Use quotation marks for the dialogue and include brief descriptions of their actions."
10. Iterate and refine: Don't be afraid to experiment with different phrasings or structures. If you don't get the desired result, try rephrasing your prompt.
Example: If "Explain quantum computing" doesn't give you the desired level of detail, try "Explain quantum computing in simple terms, focusing on how it differs from classical computing and its potential real-world applications."
